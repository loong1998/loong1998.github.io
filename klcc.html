Kuala Lumpur City Center (KLCC)

Just as its name proposes, there is only a single way to describe KLCC The Heart of Kuala Lumpur. KLCC is the home to probably the most famous landmarks in Malaysia, making it a mainstream tourist destination. With a large group of alternatives to choose from, for example, sightseeing, dining, art, culture, shopping and entertainment.

Portrayed by its developers as A City Within A City, it offers a myriad of intriguing Attractions that never stop to wonder, Just as a lot of nightlife, dining and shopping choices. KLCC is available by public transoprt with many buses working on different routes inside the area and a couple of train stations close by as well.

It built using reinforced concrete, steel, and glass, the two towers are connected to each other by a double skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors. Visitors can make their way up here for stunning views of KL and the 6.9-hectare KLCC Park below—the views are particularly impressive at night.

While most of the floors on the towers are rented to companies—IBM, Microsoft, and Huawei Technologies all have offices here—the bottom floors of the towers are reserved for Suria KLCC, one of the largest shopping centers in Malaysia. With over 300 stores, an art gallery, and even space for a Philharmonic Hall, this retail and entertainment space will keep visitors occupied for hours.
