Malay Cuisine

Malay food is strong, spicy and sweet-smelling, joining the rich tastes of the numerous spices and flavors usually found in Southeast Asia. It is one of three major cuisines in Malaysia and along with Chinese and Indian food, persistently enchant guests to the country with its fantastic assortment and flavors.

Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a Malaysian dish that comprises of colored rice that is served close by different accompaniments. In spite of the fact that it can shift in shading, the rice in nasi kerabu is generally blue since it is cooked along with petals of rhe butterfly pea flower.

The essential accompaniments to rice are vegetables, for example, long beans, bean sprouts, cabbage or cucumbers that are liberally preparesd with new spices. Other typical accompaniments are salted eggs, fried fish or chicken, sambal, fermented fish paste, ground coconut, stuffed green chilis or fish crackers.

Nasi Lemak

There is no other dish in Malaysia is just about as acclaimed as Nasi Lemak. It consists of rice cooked in coconut milk that is generally presented with anchovies, cucumbers, peanuts and boiled eggs. The dish is rounded together with the addition of a hot spicy chili paste called Sambal.

Nasi Dagang

This is a tasty Malay dish which comprises of rice that is coconut milk and for the most part served accompanied by spicy fish curry. It is customarily connected with eastern pieces of the country, essentially the districts of Kelantan and Terengganu, alongside Southern Thailand.

Nasi Dagang is depending upon the territory, it can be prepared with husked red rice or the blend of jasmine and glutinous white ruce. It is delighted in as a generous breakfast that comes served soaked in flavor-packed fish curry and it is generally complemented by pickled vegetables, boiled eggs, toasted coconut, sambal and fish crackers.
