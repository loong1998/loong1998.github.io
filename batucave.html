Batu Caves

Batu Caves, one of the most frequented tourist desitination in Kuala Lumpur, is a limestone slope involving 3 significant caverns and various more smallest one. It is situated around 11 kilometers toward the north of Kuala Lumpur, this 100-years-old sanctuary highlights icons and sculptures raised inside the primary caves and around it. Joined with limestone formations said to be around 400 miliion years of age, the sanctuary is viewed as a significant strict milestone by Hindus.

Cathedral Cave - the biggest and most well known natural cavern in Batu Caves. It houses a few Hindu sanctuaries underneath its 100-meter-high arched ceiling. At the foot of Batu Hill are two othercavern sanctuaries called the Art Gallery Cave and Musuem Cave - which houses various Hindu statues and artworks.
