Chinese Cuisine

Malaysian Chinese cuisine is gotten from the culinart traditions of Chinese Malaysian immigrants and their relatives, who have adjusted or altered their culinary customs affected by Malaysian culture just as migration examples of Chinese to Malaysia. Since by far most of Chinese Malaysians are relatives of settlers from southern China, Malaysian Chinese cuisine is transcendetly founded on a varied collection of dishes with roots fron Fujian, Cantonese, Hakka and Teochew cooking styles.

Bak Kut Teh

A really Malaysian-Chinese dish, Bah Kut Teh started from Klang Years and years prior from Klang in Selangor state. It was prepared by a stall owner under a scaffold for coolies in the region who smoked opium. As their taste buds were influenced by the drug, they did not have their sense of taste. Then, the stall owner boil pork ribs and intestines in string Chinese clinical spices, making a stew that was ready with aroma. Frpm that point, Bah Kut Teh got on and turned into a nation-wide marvel.

Chee Cheong Fun

Chee Cheong Fun is a well known as a light bite and a main meal, continfent upon the variation. Square rice sheets produced using a thick combination of rice flour and water, the liquid is poured onto a special flat pan where it is steamed to create the square rice sheets. The steamed rice sheets is rolled or folded for ease in serving and went with a sweet fermented bean sauce and chili paste.

This dish, you can discover a version of it at Dim Sum as well, yet Ipoh and Penang have somewhat various varieties of the dish, with Ipoh serving Chee Cheong Fun with a red sweet sauce while Penang serves it with a sweet, black shrimp sauce.

Tou Fu Fa

Tou Fu Fa or Douhua (in Mandarin) is a Chinese dessert made with delicate tofu eaten with a sweet syrup infusd with ginger or pandan. Some people love having it with brown sugar syrup instead of sweet syrup. Generally Tou Fu Fa will be eaten warm, but it also quite tasty after it is chilled. In the old days, Tou Fu Fa was sold by road sellers yet today you can discover this tasty dessert as a part of Dim Sum in Chinese restaurants.
